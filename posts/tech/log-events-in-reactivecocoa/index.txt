13:Td3b,<p>I used to spend a lot of time reading documentations of ReactiveCocoa (Framework overview, design guidlines, basic operators...) but, unfortunately, missed <a href="https://github.com/ReactiveCocoa/ReactiveSwift/blob/master/Documentation/DebuggingTechniques.md">this one</a>.</p>
<p>I strongly agree that debugging is not quite easy in the async world. Printing log is my common way to deal with it :D. And yes, I didn't know that RAC provides such a built-in logger.</p>
<p>Actually, I also have my own extensions (<code>logWhenStarted</code>, <code>logWhenNext</code>, <code>logWhenFailed</code>, <code>logWhenCompleted</code>...) to log events. But I have to call N functions instead. Ex:</p>
<pre><code class="hljs language-swift">producer
  .logWhenStarted(<span class="hljs-string">"Started"</span>)
	.logWhenCompleted(<span class="hljs-string">"Completed"</span>)
	.logWhenFailed(<span class="hljs-string">"Failed"</span>)
</code></pre>
